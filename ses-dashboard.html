How the score works
Component A · 50%
Drive cost per booking — billable drive time beyond the 30-minute free zone each way, at $30/hr. Route is home → job → home. Lower cost = higher score.
Component B · 30%
Home zone fit — average distance from the inspector's home to the centroid of each day's jobs. Measures whether inspectors are working in their natural geographic zone.
Component C · 20%
Job clustering — average straight-line distance between consecutive jobs on multi-job days. Tighter same-day geographic grouping = less dead travel = higher score.
Score of 100 = exactly at team average. Above 100 = more efficient than average. Below 100 = less efficient. Each component is normalized to the team average and capped at 150 to prevent outliers from dominating. Drive distances use haversine × 1.3 road factor at 35 mph — estimates only. Zip code centroids are used for all job locations; actual distances may vary by 2–5 miles within a zip code.

Important framing: The SES measures scheduling decisions, not inspector quality or effort. An inspector with a low score is being assigned to zones far from their home — this is a scheduling input, not a reflection of their performance in the field.
